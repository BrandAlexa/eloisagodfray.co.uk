Exploring the DB App and its Usability

UX

Skills

  • User Persona Creating
  • Survey Creation
  • Eye-Tracking
  • Data Anaylsis

Objective

As part of a collaborative University project, our aim was to find out how clear/easy it is for the user to find and execute common tasks on the DB app when trying to book a ticket and therefore conclude whether the app is user-friendly. Usability Testing.

Approach

We felt that the DB app was not user-friendly and wanted to test this with the use of eye tracking. We believed that the best users to test this on were international students who have little or no experience with the app so that they have not already learnt how to navigate around the app through experience. We then created personas to better understand our users and created scenarios for the users to test on the app while using eye-tracking glasses. We created three different scenarios to test different features of the app. We then analysed the results of the eye tracking data along with a survey we created to see how easy it was for the user to buy a ticket on the DB app based on the scenario given to them.

Outcome

The result was that there was a recurring issue with users being unable to navigate to the ‘Search Options’ section on the DB app and make adjustments to their search criteria. This could be seen in the data collected from the eye tracking, with, for instance, one user not navigating to this till their 99th gaze. At the time, users would be more motivated to search around the app for the function due to the 9 Euro ticket making it in their interest to navigate to the Regional Transport only. The next stage would be to contact DB with these results and work together on improving their UX based on these results.

Reflection

This was a very interesting user research project that allowed me to gain a greater understanding of the importance of UX in designing an app. This is because if the UX of an app or website is poor, it will have an effect on the business's sales too. If I were to pursue this further, I would consider using the data we collected, as well as gathering other data, to make mock-ups that could be A/B tested.
