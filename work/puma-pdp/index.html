PUMA Elevated Product Detail Page

UX/UI

Skills

  • User Research
  • Wireframing
  • Mock-ups

Objective

The aim was to ensure there was a difference between buying a regular product and a limited-edition product and therefore make the experience for the customer buying these products different. This would be achieved by creating an elevated PDP template that can be reused for different collaborations.

Approach

One main inspiration for this was from a Sanity article on ‘5 Ways to Make Your E-commerce Product Pages Pop’. I first researched how other companies in e-commerce have created their elevated PDPs. I then conducted some UX research and looked at what other UX articles suggested. Once I determined what was needed, I began to make wireframes before moving to low and high-fidelity mock-ups. One key UX research that led my design was from Baymard's Institute which said, â€œMany participants during testing commented on how important product images were to their product investigation strategy. As one participant said, “I guess for me the most important information is the picture.” I kept the main functions of the page the same for predictability, such as add to cart and sizing selector. However, I added a hero image at the start to bring the page to life, as well as adding other media to the page, such as videos to empathise the brand story and what else is in the collaboration.

Outcome

I created an elevated PDP, which makes the product feel limited edition and price justifiable. This was with PUMA's Perks and Mini's collaboration, but it can also work as a template and be reused for other elevated PDPs in the future.
