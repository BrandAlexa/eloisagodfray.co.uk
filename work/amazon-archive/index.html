Collections and Archives Exploring the Amazon Rainforest

UX/UI

Software

  • Adobe XD
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Objective

The brief for this project was to code an archive of images that were sorted into three categories but together developed a collection.

Approach

When we say “Amazon”, we no longer think about the largest rainforest in the world, along with the huge ecosystems within it. Instead, we think of Amazon, the multinational corporation. I, therefore, wanted to shed light on the Amazon rainforest and the people who call it home. I selected three tribes in the Amazon Rainforest and researched their stories before creating an image archive. To emphasise the importance of this story, I focused on the users' journey and made this as interactive yet emotive as possible. As the audience was broader for this project, I was able to regularly receive user feedback throughout my low and high-fidelity mock-ups through to my final outcome.

Outcome

I coded a responsive collection of images showing the amazon rainforest, the destruction that it faces, and the effect this has on the people who live there. The collection is arranged to tell the story of the importance of the amazon, how it is being destroyed and then exploring the tribes that live there and how they have been affected. By controlling the order of the archives, I was able to take the user on an emotive narrative that focused their attention on the stories.

Reflection

Reflecting on this project, I was happy with the outcome. I was able to gain a greater understanding of user journeys and the importance of it what the user takes away from the experience. I was also able to gain experience with coding in JavaScript to create a responsive archive. As this was a university project, there were time contrasts; however, if I were to pursue this further, I would add additional tribes to the archive to further expand the narrative.
