Objective

The aim of this research project was to produce two static visualisations within a set timeframe; they could work together or be contrasting views. I wanted to produce two data visualisations that provoked reflection on a more personal human-to-human level rather than just cold numbers. I was inspired by history and its importance of it. I thought that Berlin would be highly suited to the project because the two visualisations would be the same but different, similar to East and West Berlin. One city divided. I, therefore, aimed to create two visualisations that represented this divide.

Approach

I began this process by collecting inspirational data visualisation as well as researching East and West Berlin to get a general overview. Outcome

The outcome of this project was two static visualisations. The first used a mural from the Berlin wall to explain that the Berlin Wall, along with its fall, came with mixed feelings. The second visualisation represents individuals' stories of their experience of living in a divided Berlin during 1960 – 2000. These visualisations created a meaningful response from my peers and teachers, highlighting the importance of history.
